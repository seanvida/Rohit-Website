Backpacking through India- Southern Part


1. Arriving at Chikmangalur (Karnataka)

The town of coffee, misty weather and beautiful people- that's what my read of Chikmangalur was. Lush green trees set against the backdrop of majestic Mullayangiri ranges, Chikmagalur is one of the most popular spots for trekkers, nature enthusiasts, thrill-seekers, and explorers.

2. Arriving at Managlore

The town famous in India for producing thousands of doctors each year. Mangalore is famous for it's laid back yet a commercial vibe, nestled between Arabian Sea and western Ghat, it serves you some of the best sea food. It is also an important industrial, commercial, educational and healthcare hub of the state of Karnataka.

3. Destination de Coorg, Madikeri

Madikeri or more famously known as Coorg is the prime hill station of Southern India. It has a very nice and peculiar vibe to it. The entire terrain is full of misty hills overlooking multiple brooks and waterfalls. A go-to place for any nature lover. My trip here was exploring the town in an out on a motorbike, meeting people, relishing local food and brewing conversations over a cup of hot filter coffee!

4. Beautiful Kodaikanal

Kodaikanal or more commonly abbrevated Kodai is a hill town famous for numerous beautiful surrounding villages making osme of the best home-made chocolates. Kodaikanal is a place you can go to take a break from the rigours of daily city life, and this hill station lets you sit back and connect with nature as you head out on biking or trekking trails or take a stroll through the vast forests surrounding the town.

5. Paradise de Ooty

Nestled in the lush green Nilgiri hills, inundated with tea gardens, gushy waterfalls, country lanes, and charming architecture, Ooty is the perfect respite everyone. Popular among explorers, Ooty allures its visitors with the panoramic views of the Nilgiri mountains. The Nilgiri mountain railway is the steepest track in all of Asia and that is also where alot of tea plantation happens.
