Backpacking through India-Eastern and Western Part


1. Nort of Goa

Goa is not a place, but an emotion. This is how conversation brew in and around India: Hey! It's my birthday- Should we go to Goa?, Bro- getting bored, can we do Goa?, I want to get married - can we choose destination de Goa? This is one place full of party spots, shacks on the beach playing osme real good music, cafes serving some really nice multi cultural food. Goa has and carries something for each of us.

2. Arriving at South of Goa

South Goa known for its relaxing vibes, grooves of coconut giving you respite from the chimney hot sun, take a spot at one of the shacks by the beach- see the waves take big heights and then wash away in the moist sand. Lie down, unwind and breath- South Goa is a do-nothing and yet do it all place. Naturally crafted with Palolem beach, Cola beach, Agonda beach, it's a beautiful place to take a break at.

3. Arriving at Gokarna

Pristine beaches and breathtaking landscapes, Gokarna is a Hindu pilgrimage town in Karnataka and a newly found hub for beach lovers and hippies. Situated on the coast of Karwar, every year Gokarna welcomes hordes of tourists from around the world in search of sanctity and relaxation. The out-of-town beaches like Kudle beach and Om Beach are a different contrast to life inside the town.

4. Beautiful Gangtok

Alike any other commercial hill town, Gangtok enjoys thousand of tourist everyday in the peak months. Capital of Sikkim, a state of North-Eastern India, Gangtok serves as a middle point for travel junkies, who are set out to explore the offbeaten paths and travel the outskirts of the country.

5. Pitstop- Lachen, lachung

The journey of Gangtok to Gurudongmar lake is a treachorous one, full of surprises and tough terrains. Sore legs get the respite while you pitstop at Lachen. It is a town in Mangan District in the Indian state of Sikkim. The name Lachen means "big pass". The town is being promoted as a tourist destination by the Sikkimese government. The town forms the base to the Chopta Valley and Gurudongmar Lake. An annual yak race, the Thangu is held here in summer. Lachen has its unique form of local self governance called the “Dzumsa”. Every household is a member of this traditional administrative system, this institution is in charge of governing and organizing activities within the village.

6. Destno' Meghalaya!

Oh, you beauty! The words are still etched in my head, till date, these were the words that i uttered the moment I travelled few kilometeres ahead of Assam-Meghalaya into the lush green state of Shillong, I could truly appreciate the naturaly beauty of Meghalaya. The unforgettable landscapes of Meghalaya will stay in your heart, long after your journey is over. The table-top heights of Sohra (Cherrapunjee) are brimful with waterfalls, streams, and forest trails. Gaze at the Bangladeshi plains from the many vantage points on the state’s southern ridges.
